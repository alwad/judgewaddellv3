## High School

Highlands High School, Class of 2008

Brady played center for the Bluebirds football team and was elected captain his senior year. A real leader, Brady was a Freshman and Freshman Football Mentor in 2007–08 and received the Leader of Champions award in 2008.

## College

University of Kentucky — received both his B.A. and Master's Degrees from UK, in English and Educational Administration. He tutored students at Henry Clay High School, worked in the front office of the Lexington Legends (AA) baseball club, and completed his student teaching in the Fayette County Public School System.

## After Graduation

In 2013, Brady became the first Waddell Scholar to accept a full-time teaching position in the Ft. Thomas system, teaching English and AP English courses at Highlands. He is also Head Freshman Football Coach. Brady and his wife Ashley have two children, Jarrett and Eloise.

In recalling his own formative years, Brady notes the "outstanding education the Highlands teachers and staff gave me in preparing me for writing and planning for exams," as well as the lesson "that friends and outside influences are just as important as having a good education. The social experience I had at Highlands made it a seamless transition from high school to college. Being a part of the football team was also huge for me — football made me mentally and physically tough while also affording me lifelong friends."
