A statue of Dr. Homer Rice was unveiled outside Bobby Dodd Stadium at Georgia Tech, joining bronze memorials to John Heisman and Bobby Dodd — cementing his place among the most celebrated figures in Georgia Tech athletic history.

Dr. Rice served as Athletic Director at Georgia Tech after a distinguished career as a player, assistant coach, head coach, and athletic administrator at the high school, college, and professional levels. He is credited with transforming Georgia Tech athletics through his *Total Person* philosophy, a comprehensive leadership development program now active in dozens of colleges and universities.

## A Connection to Judge Waddell

What many who attended the unveiling may not know is that the seeds of Dr. Rice's philosophy were planted decades earlier on the practice fields of Highlands High School in Fort Thomas, Kentucky — where he was coached and mentored by Judge C.W. Waddell.

Judge Waddell later hired Rice to succeed him as head football coach at Highlands. The values Rice carried throughout his career — that athletics, academics, and character are inseparable — were values the Judge lived every day.

## Remembering Dr. Rice

Homer Rice, the principal founder of the Judge Waddell Endowed Scholarship Fund, passed away on June 10, 2024. His lifelong devotion to the Judge's memory and his tireless advocacy for this scholarship are the foundation on which this fund rests.

We are grateful for everything Dr. Rice built — and proud to carry his work forward.
