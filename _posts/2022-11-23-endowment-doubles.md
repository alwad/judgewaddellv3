The Ewell "Judge" Waddell Endowed Scholarship Fund has received gifts from the Waddell family that will double the size of the scholarship's endowment. According to Fund Chairman William Waddell, $150,000 will be received immediately, bringing the total endowment to over $300,000 — permanently funding the current level of scholarship spending.

The Waddell Scholarship was established in 2004 as a tribute to Highlands head football coach and later Superintendent of Fort Thomas Schools, Ewell E. "Judge" Waddell. Coach Waddell was a beloved and respected figure in Fort Thomas from 1940 until his untimely death in 1967. Recipients are chosen on the basis of a sincere interest in teaching and coaching, and demonstrated character and leadership ability. More than $50,000 in scholarship awards have been made since the fund's inception.

Dale Mueller, former Highlands coach and athletic director and member of the Scholarship Advisory Committee, said the scholarship and ones like it are vitally important in encouraging Highlands seniors toward teaching and coaching careers. "Our young people deserve the best teacher-coaches possible," he noted, "and this is an ingredient in making that happen."

We are deeply grateful to the Waddell family for this extraordinary expression of commitment to Judge Waddell's legacy and to the future of Highlands student-athletes.
