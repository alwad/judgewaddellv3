Our Namesake

Judge C.W. Waddell

Coach. Teacher. Mentor. The man who shaped generations of student-athletes at Highlands High School in Fort Thomas, Kentucky.

"Help encourage the teachers, coaches and mentors so important to the next generation."

A Life Devoted to Young People

Judge C.W. Waddell was a fixture of Northern Kentucky athletic life for decades. As head football coach at Highlands High School in Fort Thomas, Kentucky, he built a program distinguished not just by wins and championships, but by the character of the men who played for him.

His players went on to successful careers in every field — but perhaps his most lasting legacy is the remarkable number who became coaches and educators themselves, carrying forward the values he modeled every day: discipline, teamwork, intellectual rigor, and deep personal integrity.

Homer Rice and the "Total Person"

Among his most celebrated proteges was Homer Rice, whom Judge Waddell coached at Highlands and later hired to succeed him as head football coach. Rice went on to a distinguished career at the high school, college, and professional levels, eventually serving as Athletic Director at Georgia Tech — where he is honored with buildings bearing his name and a statue alongside John Heisman and Bobby Dodd outside the stadium.

Rice became the author of several books and developed the Total Person concept, a sports leadership program now active in dozens of colleges and universities. That philosophy has its roots in what Judge Waddell taught on the fields of Fort Thomas, Kentucky.

Homer Rice, principal founder of this scholarship, died on June 10, 2024. His lifelong devotion to the Judge's memory and values is the spirit behind everything this fund does.

The Highlands Tradition

Highlands High School in Fort Thomas has a rich athletic and academic tradition. Judge Waddell was central to building that culture — a place where excellence in the classroom and on the field were considered not competing priorities, but complementary ones.

The scholarship bearing his name seeks to continue that tradition by supporting student-athletes who embody what he believed in, and who intend to pass those values to the next generation as teachers and coaches.
